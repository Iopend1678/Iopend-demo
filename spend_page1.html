Concentration Risk Analysis
Gate Drivers — HIGH concentration risk Infineon holds 56% share ($6.2M) following addition of ADI ($0.9M, 8%). While ADI's entry modestly reduces IFX concentration from 61%, IFX remains the dominant supplier. Further qualification of TI or STMicro recommended to reduce concentration below 50%.
PMIC — MEDIUM concentration risk NXP leads at 49% ($5.3M). Spend is spread across 4 suppliers but NXP share is approaching threshold. NXP recovery trajectory improving — leverage window closing. Renegotiate volume agreements in H1 2026.
LDO — LOW concentration risk TI leads at 53% ($3.2M) but LDO is commodity-adjacent with strong multi-source availability from all four suppliers. HHI of 1,940 is below risk threshold. Maintain current distribution.

Manufacturing Model — Make vs Buy
Supplier Model Frontend (Wafer Fab) Backend (Assembly/Test)
TI
 IDM All internal. 300mm primary (Richardson, Sherman, Lehi). Closing last 2 × 150mm fabs. 40% lower cost vs 200mm. Mix of internal (Philippines, Malaysia) and external subcontractors. Majority internal per 10-K.
NXP
 Hybrid IDM Own 8" fabs: Nijmegen, Chandler, Austin. SSMC JV (61.2% NXP, TSMC partner) in Singapore. No owned 300mm — via VSMC & ESMC JVs under construction. Mix of internal and external. Assembly sites in Taiwan, Thailand, Malaysia, China.
IFX
 IDM+ Primarily internal. Kulim 300mm (Malaysia) ramping. Dresden Smart Power Fab under construction. In ESMC JV with TSMC/NXP/Bosch. Mix of internal (Batam, Melaka, Bangkok, Singapore, Cegled) and external. Some outsourced OSAT.
STM
 IDM All internal. 14 owned manufacturing sites. 200mm and 300mm (Agrate F3, Crolles F2). Crolles 300mm JV with GlobalFoundries for advanced CMOS. Mix of internal (Philippines, Malta, Morocco, Malaysia) and external subcontractors for standard packaging.
ADI
 Fab-lite >50% of wafers from TSMC and others. Own fabs: Wilmington MA, Beaverton OR, Limerick Ireland — proprietary bipolar/CMOS processes. Internal assembly at Penang, Malaysia. External OSAT for majority of volume.
Key observations
· TI & STM are true IDMs — highest supply chain control and lowest dependency on third-party foundries.
· NXP SSMC JV with TSMC creates indirect Taiwan Strait exposure for power management products.
· IFX Kulim 300mm ramp (Malaysia) strengthens capacity but increases SE Asia geopolitical concentration.
· ADI >50% TSMC reliance — highest foundry concentration risk; explicitly flagged in ADI 10-K as a material risk factor.
✅ Manufacturing model and fab locations confirmed from company 10-K / 20-F filings. Specific internal vs outsourced percentages are not publicly disclosed and are not shown.
