● Procurement Playbook — STM PMIC / LDO
Timing
Initiate renegotiation in Q1–Q2 2026 before inventory correction completes (end Q2 per CEO). Every quarter of delay reduces leverage as gross margin recovers toward year-end.
Pricing Target
Lead with Source Engine price-down signal as market benchmark. Target 8–12% reduction on PMIC/LDO. Gate Drivers: 5–8% given fewer alternatives. Use IFX and TI quotes as reference — both stable lead times and available.
Volume Lever
Offer 12-month volume commitment in exchange for price reduction. P&D segment needs loading — a committed annual volume is a direct contribution to their fixed cost absorption problem.
Payment Terms
STM DPO is 70 days — they are already stretching payables. Request extended payment terms (Net 60 → Net 75) and early payment discount. Their cash position is thin; DPO extension is a cost-free ask.
Walk-Away Signal
Use Gate Driver business as the credible walk-away signal. IFX is a direct substitute at stable lead times. Shifting 20% of Gate Driver spend to IFX in Q1 creates urgency without disrupting supply.
Risk Mitigation
Do not over-concentrate in STM despite leverage. Maintain dual-source qualification for top 5 PMIC parts. STM manufacturing reshaping adds execution risk through 2026 — hedge with TI or IFX buffer stock on critical parts.
