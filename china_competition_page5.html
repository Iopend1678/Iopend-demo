Why This Matters for Procurement
Chinese domestic PMIC and LDO suppliers have been gaining share in China's industrial and automotive markets rapidly since 2021 — accelerated by the chip shortage and localisation pressure. For Western buyers, the immediate question is not "should we buy from Chinese suppliers" but rather: how much are Chinese suppliers displacing Western incumbents in the China market (reducing those suppliers' China revenue and thus increasing their pressure to negotiate globally), and are any Chinese players ready to qualify in Western supply chains? The answers differ by product category and by application grade.
⚠
China Anti-Dumping Investigation — September 2025 (Active)
China's Ministry of Commerce (MOFCOM) initiated an anti-dumping investigation in September 2025 specifically targeting gate driver ICs and general-purpose interface chips imported from the United States. The investigation covers products from TI and ADI specifically and runs until at least September 2026. MOFCOM stated that US mature-node IC import volume increased 37% from 2022–2024 while import prices fell 52%, which "lowered and suppressed domestic sales prices." This is a direct policy tailwind for Chinese gate driver suppliers including Southchip and SG Micro. Procurement implication: if this results in tariffs on US-origin gate driver ICs sold into China, it changes the cost calculus for buyers with China-based manufacturing.
✅ Confirmed · MOFCOM announcement Sep 2025 via SemiWiki / SCMP ✅ Products: Low-side, half-bridge, isolated gate driver ICs

External Threat Assessment
BYD Semiconductor is fundamentally a captive supplier to BYD's own EV production. Its strategic value is cost reduction and supply security for BYD — not competing in the open market. Some external qualification is occurring but volume to non-BYD customers is minimal and not the strategic priority. For Western procurement teams, BYD Semiconductor is not a realistic near-term alternative. Its primary relevance is as evidence that Chinese IDM capability is growing — and as a signal that BYD is reducing its dependence on Western PMIC/gate driver suppliers for its EV platforms.

Technology Maturity & Assessment

Procurement Risk & Opportunity Matrix
China Market Revenue Risk to Western Suppliers
Chinese PMIC/LDO suppliers are displacing TI, NXP and IFX in the China domestic market — particularly in consumer and industrial. This reduces Western suppliers' China revenue, which increases their pressure to be competitive globally. This is already reflected in the negotiation leverage scores for STM and IFX — China volume loss is part of the utilisation problem.
🔢 Analysis — derived from supplier revenue trends and domestic market reports
Gate Driver Anti-Dumping — China Manufacturing Impact
If China imposes anti-dumping tariffs on US-origin gate driver ICs (TI, ADI), buyers with China-based manufacturing will face a cost increase on US-sourced gate drivers. This creates an incentive to qualify Chinese alternatives (Southchip, SG Micro) for China-market production while maintaining Western suppliers for export markets. Dual-sourcing by geography may become necessary.
✅ MOFCOM investigation confirmed Sep 2025 — gate drivers in scope
Western Supply Chain Entry — Timeline Assessment
Silergy is the most likely near-term entrant into Western industrial supply chains for standard LDO and DC/DC. SG Micro is a longer path given less automotive focus. BYD Semiconductor is captive. Southchip is automotive-qualified in China but Western OEM approval is likely 2–3+ years away. SiC/GaN gate drivers: not credible within a 5-year horizon from any Chinese supplier.
◆ Working assumption — timeline based on typical automotive qualification cycles
Geopolitical Export Control Risk
US export controls on semiconductor equipment have limited Chinese fabs' ability to advance beyond mature nodes (~28nm). For PMIC and LDO (which use BCD processes at 130–180nm), this is not a barrier — the relevant nodes are outside export control scope. For advanced SiC/GaN gate drivers, equipment access constraints may slow Chinese development further.
🔢 Analysis — based on publicly reported US export control scope (Commerce BIS)
TSMC Dependency of Chinese Fabless Players
Silergy and SG Micro both use TSMC as their primary foundry. This means they share the same Taiwan geopolitical risk as ADI — and are subject to the same US export controls that restrict TSMC from supplying Chinese companies with advanced nodes if those controls were extended. At current mature node usage (BCD 130–180nm), this is not a near-term risk, but it is a structural dependency.
✅ TSMC reliance confirmed — SG Micro website, Silergy 2023 annual report
Procurement Action — What to Watch
Watch now: MOFCOM anti-dumping ruling (due ~Sep 2026) — determines if tariffs apply to your China-manufactured gate driver purchases.

Evaluate: Silergy and Southchip for simple LDO (100–500mA) in China-market industrial products — lowest risk, least qualification friction.

Do not qualify yet: Any Chinese supplier for automotive-grade PMIC (ASIL-D), SiC/GaN gate drivers, or complex multi-rail PMICs in safety-critical applications.
